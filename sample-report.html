§ 1 · Executive summary

The unit passes, with two conditions worth pricing in.

Mechanically the instrument is healthy. The binary pump, autosampler and column compartment all hold their respective specs across the protocol window — the unit ran reference samples for 24 hours without intervention.

Two findings warrant a price adjustment rather than a walk: the DAD lamp is at 71 % remaining hours (well above the 50 % we treat as a hard floor, but a replacement is within 12 months at normal duty cycle); and the outlet check valve on pump A shows a 3 % pressure ripple at high flow, marginal but recoverable with a $1 200 part.

Recommended bid is $75 500 against the listed ask of $84 000 — a 10.1 % delta that accommodates a DAD lamp replacement, an outlet check valve, and a $400 column shipping buffer.

§ 2 · Methodology

Five test envelopes, run against the OEM spec sheet.

The unit was inspected at the seller's bench in Boston over two working days. Power, solvent, gas, and reference samples were arranged with the vendor ahead of the visit. No part was removed; covers were opened for serials, firmware, and visual inspection only.

Risks & what we did not test

The instrument was not run with the buyer's intended HILIC column; if the application requires sub-2 µm phases at > 1 000 bar, repeat the pump pulsation test at the buyer's site after delivery. We did not inspect the autosampler thermostatic option (not fitted on this unit per OEM serial).

Recommend post-arrival re-verification at the buyer's site once the lamp and check valve are installed. We can dispatch the same inspector; budget $1 800 for a one-day return visit.
